WHAT'S NEXT?

The 2023 FIFA Women's World Cup is the tenth edition of the quadrennial international women's football tournament organized by FIFA. It is scheduled to take place in Australia and New Zealand from July 10 to August 20, 2023.

The tournament will feature 32 teams from around the world, with matches held in 10 host cities across both countries. The tournament format will consist of a group stage, followed by knockout rounds leading up to the final match.

The Women's World Cup is the most prestigious international tournament in women's football, and is a showcase for the sport's top players and teams. The defending champions are the United States, who won the 2019 tournament held in France. The tournament has seen increasing popularity and interest in recent years, as women's football continues to grow and gain recognition around the world.
